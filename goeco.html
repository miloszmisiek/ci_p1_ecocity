how to segrate waste ?

plastic bags and bottles, food containers, cleaning/cosmetic packagings, bottle tops and lids, milk/juice cartons, soda/tin cans, aluminium foil

PLASTICS & METAL

do not put in:
hazardous waste such as expired medications, batteries, motor oil and its packaging, car parts, paint or varnish cans

paper bags and packages, cartons and paperboard, catalogs, leaflets, old magazines, notebooks, books, stationery (office paper), wrapping paper

PAPER

do not put in:
greasy paper, pads, paper towels, used tissues, coated paper, milk or juice cartons, cement sacks

food and beverage bottles and jars, cosmetics packaging

GLASS

do not put in:
porcelain, pottery, crystals, flower pots, bulbs, window panes, table glassware, eye-wear, heat-proof glass, prescription bottles

food lefovers, fruits and vegetables, green waste (tree and shrub branches), grass, leaves, flowers

BIO-WASTE

do not put in:
animal bones or excrement, ash, soil, stones or dust, particle board, treated wood

animal bones and excrements, egg shells, disposable diapers, contaminated paper, ceramics and textiles, ash (cold), regular filament bulbs

OTHER-WASTE

do not put in:
batteries, bulbs, fluorescent or neon lamps, paints and varnish residues, waste electrical/electronic equipment

change starts with you !

GO CYCLING

"Approximately, cycling 10 km each way to work would save 1,500 kg of greenhouse gas emissions each year. With moderate increase of the use of bicycles, cycling could save 6 to 14 million tons of CO2 and 700 million to 1.6 billion gallons of fuel each year." (REID Bikes)

NATURE IS POWER

"Electricity from renewable energy sources produces between 90-99% less greenhouse gases (GHGs) compared with coal-fired plants and causes 70-90% less pollution. Focusing on renewable energy sources other than fossil fuels and coals might help in avoiding environmental impacts, specifically from air pollution and GHGs." (ADEC Innovations)
